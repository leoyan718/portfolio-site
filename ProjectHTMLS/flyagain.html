The Boy Who Learned to Fly Again (2025)


AI Storytelling Short

Videos made by Kling 2.5 AI Video Engine

Edited in Adobe Premiere Pro and Adobe After Effects






Artist Statement

Everyone carries weight and as that weight accumulates, it can become overwhelming which causes the things that lifts us up to fall. The Boy Who Learned to Fly Again are separate clips made from prompting Kling 2.5 and shows a hot air balloon falling down from the sky mainly due to the overwhelming amount of weight stored.

The boy waking up from the crash finds himself stranded and lost. He sees other balloons gradually hovering in the sky above him and wonders why he couldn’t be like one of them and do the same. Broken and hurt physically as well as emotionally, the boy learns from his mistake and starts moving all the weight, storing all the boxes away in order for the balloon to be lighter. After doing that, he feels more relieved and starts fixing the balloon to the point where he could start flying again. And now ever since he dropped off all the weight and left it behind, the balloon stays in the air this time without resistance, flying with the others.

The concept behind this is thinking about the hot air balloon as myself. Overfilling myself with a lot of weight would cause me to fall down either because there is that feeling of being overwhelmed, hurt, or stressed. So therefore, the boy putting away the boxes, otherwise known as the weight in order to ascend again is a way of saying to let go of the things that have hurt you in the past and be happy or healed again.
