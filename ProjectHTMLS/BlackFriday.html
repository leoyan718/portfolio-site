Silence on Black Friday (2025)


Sensorial Ethnography Video Project

Edited in Adobe Premiere Pro






Artist Statement

For this sensorial ethnography project titled Silence on Black Friday, I filmed it during my Thanksgiving break in San Francisco on Black Friday. The main concept I thought of was recording three clips that may contrast the idea of Black Friday. Since Black Friday usually consists of lots of people in the city, loud conversations, and lots of activities, I wanted to capture clips that show the complete opposite. A place that popped into my mind immediately was San Francisco Shopping Center located downtown. This shopping mall used to be popular but now is a complete dead mall or ghost town due to its decline in the early 2020s with about 90% of the shops closing down. I remember going there when I was a teenager and being amazed by how massive and full of life it was but for today, it’s mostly empty and closed off shops with barely any people. So overall, through these three shots I filmed during that afternoon, the first being in a subway station on my way to the mall builds up the other two by showing how little human activity is also there. The second shot shows the atrium located at the top floor of the mall, a wide and vast space, and how little to no human activity is there as well. And to finish it off, the third shot shows escalators of the mall still operating but barely anybody riding them up and down, reflecting on how the mall still runs even though nobody is there.
